Overview

As military service members transition to civilian life, they often encounter challenges such as industry stereotypes, limited awareness of opportunities, and skill gaps for civilian careers. In this scenario, veterans encounter impediments that delay the natural progression of their careers, restricting them to specific roles and career paths. Veterans may also lack awareness of the dynamic demands of the civilian job market, resulting in suboptimal career choices.As military service members transition to civilian life, they often encounter challenges such as industry stereotypes, limited awareness of opportunities, and skill gaps for civilian careers. In this scenario, veterans encounter impediments that delay the natural progression of their careers, restricting them to specific roles and career paths. Veterans may also lack awareness of the dynamic demands of the civilian job market, resulting in suboptimal career choices. To bridge the gap between military skills and civilian roles, ISB Executive Education and CommandHQ collaborated to co-create a bespoke programme facilitating the integration of veterans into the workforce. This 5-month learning intervention equips veterans with the skills, mindset, and competencies required for a confident transition to job roles of their choice. The programme helps learners align their life experiences with job responsibilities and leverage their competencies for success in the corporate world. The programme participants will acquire business, managerial, and leadership skills to build a personal brand, manage teams, make informed decisions, and develop corporate strategies. The programme also enables the learners to strategically navigate the corporate world, reshape industry perceptions, and break free from stereotypes. The programme will conclude with an assessment for certification purposes, which will be followed by a Graduation Ceremony.

Programme Curriculum

The programme curriculum is organised into modules with rich pedagogy consisting of campus immersions, faculty presentations, asynchronous modules, group discussions, and case studies scheduled across five months.

Module 3: Personal Leadership

The road to becoming the leader of choice is certainly not easy. Disruptions & changing dynamics every now & then can be overwhelming for individuals. This module is designed to help leaders embrace challenges and opportunities associated with a leadership role. It will help them develop the awareness, knowledge, skills and attributes necessary to succeed as leaders. They will gain a concrete understanding of the nuances and mindsets required to meet the demands of the new role.

Module 4: Economics for Managers

To deliver business excellence & sustained growth in the highly competitive and dynamic business ecosystem, leaders must constantly update their understanding of what is happening inside and outside their organisation. A thorough understanding of the economic indicators will help managers manage risks and identify opportunities given the economic environment. This module will help participants develop a business edge for analysing the impact of the economy on their organisation and enhance their decision-making capabilities.

• Describe opportunity cost and its importance in decision-making
• Define and explore the law of demand
• Understand the law of supply
• Learn profit maximisation and how to plan to achieve it
• Understand game theory and the different kinds of solutions it depicts
• Explore the concept of oligopolies and how it affects various markets

Module 5: Strategy and Execution

A strategy is only as successful as the execution that follows it. The strategic formulation is typically the anchor that companies use to achieve their vision and objective. Yet, most of these companies often encounter situations where their strategic plans fall short. The problem usually lies in execution & articulation, which requires a dynamic orchestra between internal and external stakeholders.

• Explore the connection between strategy failure and organisational misalignment

• Develop a framework for organisational alignment and execution

• Build critical dimensions of personal leadership required to effect organisational changes and execute strategy collaboratively

• Learn to mobilise resources and capabilities towards an aligned objective

• Devise a long-term plan to follow through with the strategic execution process & measure the success

Module 8: Data to Decisions

With the digital revolution, data can be tapped at scale to drive improvements in growth and performance across product development, operations, market penetration, etc. However, without the right tools and strategies, organisations face a lag in connecting and integrating their data to generate strategic insights. This module aims at emphasising the role of business data analytics as a decision-making paradigm that can unlock huge data dividend for any company. To that end, it will deliver an understanding of

• The anatomy of data-driven decision making and data-driven organisations

• Explore a range of techniques such as clustering, recommendation algorithms, optimization, forecasting, NLP, etc. that leverage both structured and unstructured data to derive strategic insights

• Assess synergies between analytics and cloud computing to drive new business models powered by smart automation and proactive interventions

• Use predictive analytics, including, use cases of AI, neural networks, and deep learning for creating business value

Module 9: Emerging Tech Shaping the Future

We're living in times of transformation. The pace of tech evolution is becoming difficult for businesses to keep up with. However, leaders can't afford to be left behind. This accelerated pace of change — complemented by the technologies we use and evidenced through disruptive social, economic and political behaviours — needs a kind of different leadership. So how can we make decisions to benefit our stakeholders and communicate credibly with them given this emergent environment? This module will help you develop a 360 - degree view of the tech ecosystem & its impact on business practices.

• Develop a foundational understanding of emerging technologies and how to forecast their adoption, socialization and impact

• Understand the What, Why & How of different emerging technology: Quantum Computing in the Future, AI and ML, Gen AI, IoT, RPA, 5G, Edge computing & Blockchain

• Learn about the real-life success & failure implementation instances for these technologies

It is also about establishing trust & harmony among the people of the team & setting up a culture of collaboration & growth. This module focuses on different aspects of a team structure, synergy, mutual trust and much more, which will aid the managers in ensuring their teams reach new heights. • Explore frameworks to understand team creation and manage team functioning effectively

• Help foster talent for future roles Module 11: Operations Management

Operations strategy consists of the strategic use of functions such as manufacturing, supply chain, or service provision. Traditionally, these areas have been viewed as narrow, functional areas, and management of them was based on some simple criteria such as cost minimization.

More recently, managers and business observers have understood that operations must be managed in the broader context of business strategy. In this sense, decisions on operations capabilities must fit and be consistent with the business strategy and consider the competitive environment

• Familiarize yourself with the fundamental concepts of Operations Management such as process flows, and performance measures

• Apply theoretical knowledge to real-world scenarios by analyzing a company case study

• Develop the ability to identify and address bottlenecks in business processes, including the concept of shifting bottlenecks

• Enhance quantitative analysis skills by working with reference data and computing performance matrices

• Apply quantitative methods such as Little's Law and Marginal Value Analysis in solving problems related to process efficiency and inventory management.

• Acquire practical knowledge in inventory management by exploring topics such as dealing with unpredictable variability, computing expected profit, and implementing marginal value analysis

• Evaluate the importance of perishable asset revenue management in optimizing inventory and understand its impact on overall operations.

Moudle 12: Dealing with Ambiguity & Change

Research shows that 70 per cent of all change management initiatives fail to reach the intended target. While planning a change is essential, the pace of an organisation’s growth is directly proportional to the openness of its leaders' approach to accepting change. The ability to bring out change smoothly and effectively is a distinguishing characteristic of successful leaders. This module will help you develop a strategic mindset for embracing and adopting change.

• Learn to identify the forces for and against change - Appreciate that change involves not only rationality but also emotions and relationships

• Using different frameworks, understand the process of change and its stages

• Learn ways to create a suitable climate for change and how to engage and enable the organisation

• Develop an understanding of the competencies required for effective change management

• Gain context-specific insights into driving change management in organisations

In the module, you will explore practical ways to explore and understand your goals from the onset of your project and consider all the elements that may affect its execution.
